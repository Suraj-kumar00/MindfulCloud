About Mental Health First Aid Hub

We provide accessible resources, tools, and support to help you navigate mental health challenges with confidence.

Our Mission

To provide accessible, evidence-based mental health resources that empower individuals to support themselves and others during times of need.

Our Approach

We combine scientific research with practical tools to create resources that are easy to use, effective, and accessible to everyone regardless of background.

Our Values

Compassion, accessibility, inclusivity, and evidence-based practice guide everything we do to ensure everyone receives the support they deserve.

4-7-8 Breathing

This technique helps reduce anxiety, manage stress, and promote better sleep. It's based on pranayama, an ancient yogic practice of breath regulation.

Step 1: Inhale

Inhale through your nose for 4 seconds.

Step 2: Hold

Hold your breath for 7 seconds.

Step 3: Exhale

Exhale completely through your mouth for 8 seconds.

Tip: Find a comfortable seated position and practice in a quiet environment for best results.

Reduce Anxiety

Controlled breathing helps activate your parasympathetic nervous system, reducing feelings of anxiety and stress.

Improve Focus

Regular breathing exercises can enhance cognitive function, concentration, and mental clarity.

Better Sleep

Practicing breathing exercises before bed can help calm your mind and prepare your body for restful sleep.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Find answers to common questions about mental health, our resources, and how to get the most out of our tools.

    Mental Health First Aid is the initial support offered to someone who may be experiencing a mental health issue or emotional distress. Similar to physical first aid, it focuses on providing assistance until appropriate professional help is received or the crisis resolves. Our hub provides resources, tools, and education to help people recognize signs of mental health concerns and respond effectively.

    No, our self-assessment is not a diagnostic tool. It's designed to help you reflect on your mental health and identify potential areas of concern, but it cannot replace a professional evaluation. The results should be used as a starting point for conversations with healthcare providers, not as a definitive diagnosis. If you're experiencing significant distress, please consult with a qualified mental health professional.

    Breathing exercises help activate your body's relaxation response by stimulating the parasympathetic nervous system. When you're anxious, your breathing typically becomes shallow and rapid, which can intensify feelings of anxiety. Controlled breathing exercises interrupt this cycle by slowing your heart rate, lowering blood pressure, and reducing stress hormones. With regular practice, these exercises can help you manage anxiety symptoms more effectively and provide a practical tool for use during stressful situations.

    We take your privacy seriously. All assessment results and saved preferences are stored locally on your device using browser localStorage. This means your data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to or stored on our servers. You can clear this data at any time by clearing your browser's localStorage or using the options provided within our tools. This approach gives you complete control over your information while still allowing you to track your progress over time.

    Our resource locator uses OpenStreetMap API to provide location-based results. While we strive for accuracy, the database of mental health resources is continuously updated, and there may occasionally be changes in service availability or contact information. We recommend contacting facilities directly to confirm details before visiting. If you notice any inaccuracies, please let us know through our contact form, and we'll update the information as quickly as possible.

    Yes! Our coping strategy cards are designed to be freely printable and shareable for educational and therapeutic purposes. Many healthcare providers, teachers, and counselors use these resources in their work. You're welcome to print multiple copies for your patients, students, or clients. We only ask that you maintain the attribution to Mental Health First Aid Hub when distributing these materials and do not sell them or claim them as your own work.

    If you or someone you know is experiencing a mental health emergency or crisis:

    • Call 988 for the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (US)
    • Text HOME to 741741 to reach the Crisis Text Line
    • Call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room if there's immediate danger
    • Contact your doctor or mental health professional
    • Use our resource locator to find crisis services near you

    Remember, our website tools are not designed for emergency situations and should not be used as a substitute for immediate professional help during a crisis.

    We value your input! You can provide feedback or suggest new features through our contact form. We regularly review user suggestions to improve our resources and add new tools that would benefit our community. While we can't implement every suggestion, we carefully consider each one and prioritize changes that will help the most people. Your experiences and ideas are essential in making Mental Health First Aid Hub more effective and accessible.
